Section 6: What Could Go Wrong — The Honest Assessment 
The series has attempted intellectual honesty throughout. The final paper would be dishonest if 
it did not apply that standard to the proposal itself. 
The capture risk. Every institution that begins with genuine integrity faces the continuous 
pressure of the interests it displaces. The current financial architecture generates extraordinary 
rents — interest payments, financing fees, currency arbitrage, sovereign risk premiums — for 
the institutions that operate it. Those institutions have the resources, the sophistication, and the 
motivation to capture the Genesis mechanisms that would displace them. The transparency 
architecture — every decision, every allocation, every Vault assessment on the blockchain in 
real time — is the primary protection. It is not a guarantee. Vigilance from citizens, economists, 
journalists, and civil society is the vigilance that no architectural mechanism can fully substitute 
for. 
The sequencing risk. The phased deployment described in Paper 8 is not caution for its own 
sake. It is the anti-inflation mechanism. Political pressure to accelerate — from populations 
whose expectations have been raised, from governments eager to access infrastructure capital, 
from the genuine urgency of conditions at Level 1 — will be continuous. The discipline of the 
sequence must hold even when it is politically inconvenient. That discipline depends on the 
transparency and accountability structures of Paper 9 functioning as designed. 
The geopolitical resistance risk. The nations that benefit most from the current architecture have 
structural incentives to resist Genesis adoption and undermine its credibility. The resistance will 
be well-resourced and sophisticated. The neutral voice of these papers — which names no 
enemy, assigns no blame, makes no accusation, and offers nothing but the data and the 
architecture — is the primary defence. A proposal that can be dismissed as ideological or 
adversarial is vulnerable. A proposal that is transparently non-profit, mathematically honest, and 
genuinely neutral has no vulnerability of that kind. 
The time risk. The most important compounding effects of the Genesis programme take 
decades to materialise. The founding documents of the Genesis non-profit make the transfer of 
authority to the full council system structural rather than discretionary. These protections are 
genuine. They are not absolute. 
These risks are stated not as reasons not to proceed but as the honest acknowledgment of 
what proceeding requires. The case for Genesis is not that it is risk-free. It is that the risks of 
attempting it are substantially lower than the risks of not attempting it — and that the trajectory 
documented in Section 2 makes the cost of inaction as clearly visible as the cost of imperfect 
action.
