The Core Thesis 
Everything is signals. Every digital system — biological sensor networks, financial rails, enterprise 
identity layers, manufacturing floors, power grids, robotic actuators, AI agents — produces behavioral 
signals as a byproduct of existing. The question is who learns to read them intelligently. 
 
AFI's fusion engine doesn't detect cyber attacks specifically. It detects behavioral anomalies in signal 
streams. The connector library determines which signals enter the engine. The detector packs 
determine which behavioral geometries the engine recognises. The fusion math underneath is domain-
agnostic by architecture — not by analogy. 
 
The fusion engine does not care what signals it fuses. It cares about behavioral geometry — 
the shape of how things move, the timing, sequence, and structural signature of normal 
versus anomalous. This is not a metaphor. It is literally how the codebase is architected.
