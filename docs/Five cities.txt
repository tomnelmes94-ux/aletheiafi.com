THE FIVE GENESIS CITIES — SITE IDENTIFICATION 
 
CITY 1: GAIA — Nubian Corridor, Northeastern Sudan 
Already fully specified. The flagship. The largest. 
 
CITY 2: TERRA — The Brazilian Cerrado Plateau 
The site: The Tocantins-Maranhão corridor in north-central Brazil. The boundary region between 
Tocantins state and Maranhão state — specifically the elevated interior plateau known as the 
MATOPIBA agricultural frontier, but away from the most ecologically sensitive areas. 
Approximately 12°S, 47°W. The area sits at 300-600 metres elevation — temperate enough to 
be genuinely liveable, flat enough to build at scale, and far enough from the Amazon basin to 
avoid the ecological complications that would make negotiation impossible. 
Why here: 
The Cerrado plateau is the water tower of South America. It contains the headwaters of the 
Araguaia-Tocantins, Paraná-Paraguay, and São Francisco river systems — three of the 
continent’s most significant watersheds. A city built here has access to surface water that no 
desert site can match. Average temperatures of 20-26°C year-round — warm but not extreme. 
The Tocantins River runs through the region providing both water supply and navigable 
waterway access. Rainfall of 1,200-1,600mm annually means the city is not dependent on 
desalination in the way Gaia is — water falls from the sky in sufficient quantity to supplement the 
river supply. 
The strategic case: The Cerrado is already being cleared at 80,000 hectares per year — mostly 
for soy and cattle ranching. The negotiation with Brazil is not “give us your pristine wilderness” 
but “give us degraded Cerrado that is already being converted to monoculture, and we will build 
something that generates more economic value and restores more ecological function than the 
soy farmer would.” That is a fundamentally easier negotiation. Brazil gets GDP growth, a new 
economic hub, Genesis infrastructure investment, and an argument to the world that it is 
developing rather than merely extracting. Genesis gets a site with rivers, rainfall, good 
temperatures, flat buildable land, and zero ocean access required. 
What it doesn’t have: Ocean access. The Atlantic port access requires rail to the coast — 
approximately 800km to the port of São Luís in Maranhão, or 1,200km to the Atlantic coast of 
Ceará. This is manageable but it is a constraint that Gaia doesn’t have. The HSR connection to 
both coasts and north-south through Brazil is the solution. The city is a continental hub rather 
than a coastal port. 
Name suggestion: Terra — Latin and Portuguese for Earth, the second Genesis city named for 
a natural element. 
Population potential: The Cerrado plateau has significant buildable land — with a 50,000 km² 
footprint and similar household mathematics applied to a South American context, a city of 
50-100 million people across generations is achievable. Smaller than Gaia by design — Terra is 
the South American anchor rather than the global flagship. 
 
CITY 3: AURORA — The Mekong Corridor, Northern Laos / Myanmar Border Region 
The site: The Upper Mekong River corridor in the border region between northern Laos and 
eastern Myanmar — specifically the Shan Plateau of Myanmar or the Phongsali Province of 
Laos. Approximately 21°N, 100°E. Elevation 800-1,200 metres — the highland plateau that 
makes this region cooler than the surrounding lowlands and genuinely comfortable year-round. 
Why here: 
Laos is the least urbanised country in Southeast Asia. Only 0.09% of its land area is developed 
urban space. The country has been actively developing from a land-locked disadvantaged 
country into a land-linked one — the China-Laos HSR opened in 2021, connecting Vientiane to 
Kunming in southern China. That existing rail infrastructure is the starting point for the Genesis 
HSR expansion. The Genesis city in Asia connects directly to China’s 50,000km HSR network 
through the Laos corridor that already exists. 
The Mekong River provides freshwater at scale. The highland plateau provides moderate 
temperatures. The proximity to China ensures the construction partnership is geographically 
optimal — China builds from the north along existing routes rather than having to ship 
equipment halfway across the world. The region sits at the intersection of Chinese, Indian, and 
Southeast Asian economic spheres — a genuinely strategic location for a continental economic 
hub. 
The political complexity: Myanmar’s military governance and Laos’s communist government are 
both sensitive negotiations. The honest acknowledgment is that this is the most politically 
complex of the five sites. The Genesis neutrality framework — no political conditions beyond the 
transparency standard — is what makes the negotiation possible. Genesis is not asking either 
government to change its system. It is offering to build a free city on land that currently produces 
little economic value, on terms that are financially beneficial to the host country and that the host 
country governs at arm’s length. 
The China dimension: The Genesis Asia city is the most important China partnership site. 
China’s influence in Laos and Myanmar is already significant — it built the Laos HSR, it has 
mineral interests in Myanmar, it has extensive investment relationships with both governments. 
The Genesis city in this corridor is not competing with Chinese influence. It is building on it. The 
partnership with China for construction and HSR is most seamlessly executed at the site where 
China’s existing infrastructure is closest. 
Population potential: The highland plateau has significant buildable area but the topography is 
more complex than the Nubian Desert or the Brazilian Cerrado. A city of 20-50 million people 
across generations is the realistic target — smaller than Gaia and Terra but strategically 
positioned at the intersection of 4 billion people in South and Southeast Asia. 
Name suggestion: Aurora — the dawn, the light. The city that represents the beginning of the 
Asian development arc. 
 
CITY 4: NOVA — The Ukrainian-Romanian Plains 
The site: The border region between western Ukraine and northeastern Romania — specifically 
the Prut River valley and the Moldovan plateau region. Approximately 47°N, 28°E. This is the 
heart of Eastern Europe’s most underutilised agricultural land — the chernozem black soil belt 
that makes Ukraine the breadbasket of Europe, with some of the most fertile land on earth and 
a population that has been declining for decades. 
Why here: 
Eastern Europe is experiencing the most severe demographic crisis on earth. Ukraine’s 
population has fallen from approximately 52 million in 1991 to approximately 37 million today — 
before the 2022 war, which accelerated emigration dramatically. Moldova is the poorest country 
in Europe with approximately 2.4 million people and a population that has been leaving for 
decades. Romania has lost approximately 4 million people to emigration since EU accession. 
The entire region represents an extraordinary concentration of available land, declining 
population, and infrastructure that was built for a much larger population base and is now 
underutilised. 
The Prut River valley where Romania and Moldova converge has flat agricultural land, the 
Danube delta 200km south providing water and port access to the Black Sea, temperate climate 
(warm summers, cold winters — genuine seasons), and the existing European rail network to 
the west. Ukraine’s agricultural productivity means food security is not a question — the black 
soil surrounding the city is the most productive farmland on earth. 
The negotiation case: This is where the political complexity is highest in one specific dimension 
— the Russia-Ukraine war and its aftermath. Any site in Ukraine or on its border carries the 
geopolitical weight of that conflict. The honest position is that the Genesis city in Eastern Europe 
may need to wait for a political settlement in Ukraine before it can be designed into that specific 
location, or it may need to shift to a purely Romanian or Bulgarian site that avoids the conflict 
zone entirely. A secondary option is the Pannonian Plain of Hungary and Serbia — flat, 
agricultural, underutilised, and politically stable. 
What makes Eastern Europe compelling regardless of the specific site: The demographic crisis 
means that the governments of this region — Romania, Bulgaria, Moldova, Serbia — are 
actively looking for reasons to attract population and economic activity. The negotiation is not 
“let us build here” but “we will bring population, economic activity, and infrastructure investment 
to a region that is emptying.” That is the strongest negotiation position of any Genesis city 
outside Africa. 
Population potential: The Eastern European city is not designed for the population scale of Gaia 
or even Terra. It is designed to be the European anchor — a city of 10-20 million people that 
provides the European HSR connection, the Genesis institutional presence on the continent, 
and the demonstration that the Genesis model works in a developed-world context as well as in 
the developing world. 
Name suggestion: Nova — new. The city that demonstrates a new beginning for a region 
defined by emigration and demographic decline. 
 
CITY 5: SOLARA — The Isthmus of Tehuantepec, Southern Mexico 
The site: The Isthmus of Tehuantepec in the states of Oaxaca and Veracruz, southern Mexico. 
Approximately 16.5°N, 95°W. The narrowest point in Mexico — only 200km between the Gulf of 
Mexico and the Pacific Ocean. 
Why here: 
The Isthmus of Tehuantepec is one of the most strategically significant geographic locations in 
the Western Hemisphere. It is the land bridge between the Atlantic and Pacific — the narrowest 
crossing point between the two oceans south of the US-Mexico border. It already has a major 
infrastructure development underway — the Interoceanic Corridor of the Isthmus of 
Tehuantepec, a Mexican government project building a freight rail corridor connecting the two 
oceans. This existing rail infrastructure is the Genesis HSR spine. 
The wind resource at Tehuantepec is extraordinary — the Tehuantepec wind corridor is one of 
the highest-capacity wind energy sites on earth. The Oaxacan wind farms in this region are 
already some of the largest in Latin America. Combined with solar, the energy picture for this 
site rivals Gaia. 
The population pressure context is directly relevant. The migration crisis from Central America 
— Honduras, Guatemala, El Salvador, Nicaragua — is driven by the economic differential 
between staying and leaving. The Genesis city at the Isthmus is positioned to close that 
differential. Not by stopping migration. By creating a place worth staying for, worth coming to, 
and worth building in — that happens to be on the route that migrants currently travel toward the 
US border. 
The Mexico partnership: Mexico has the largest Spanish-speaking population on earth, a 
significant manufacturing base, strong construction capacity, and a government that has been 
actively developing the Isthmus region for years. The Genesis partnership with Mexico is not 
asking for something new — it is accelerating and scaling something Mexico is already trying to 
do, with Genesis capital and the global partnership network behind it. 
The Atlantic-Pacific position: A city at the Isthmus is simultaneously a Gulf of Mexico port and a 
Pacific port — connected by rail across 200km of land. This is the Western Hemisphere’s 
answer to Singapore — a strategic transit and commercial hub at the intersection of two oceans 
and multiple continental trade routes. 
The Yucatan alternative: The research surfaced the Yucatan Peninsula as a secondary option 
— 181,000 km² of flat limestone terrain, coastal on three sides, low population density (5.1 
million across the three Mexican states), extraordinary cenote freshwater system underground. 
The Yucatan eliminates the hurricane risk concern of the Isthmus and provides more buildable 
land. However the limestone karst geology — the same cenote system that makes water access 
interesting — creates foundation engineering challenges for large-scale construction. The 
Isthmus is the preferred primary site. The Yucatan is noted as an alternative. 
Population potential: Southern Mexico and Central America have a combined population of 
approximately 200 million people within a 1,000km HSR radius of the Isthmus. The Genesis city 
here is the economic magnet that changes the migration equation for an entire region. A city of 
30-50 million people across generations — smaller than Gaia but transformative for the Western 
Hemisphere’s most persistent development challenge. 
Name suggestion: Solara — the sun. Named for the extraordinary solar and wind energy 
resources of the Tehuantepec corridor, and for the warmth of the cultural region it serves.
